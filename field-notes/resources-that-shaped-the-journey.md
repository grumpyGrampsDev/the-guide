## Purpose

This is not a required set of resources or a prescribed method for studying Scripture.

Scripture remains the foundation and authority.

These are the resources, practices, communities, and rhythms that have helped shape my own walk with God and my approach to reading, studying, praying, and being formed by His Word.

I didn't gather these all at once. Over time, God used different tools at different seasons to deepen my understanding of His Word.

Each layer serves a distinct purpose.

Together, they have helped me move from simply reading Scripture toward being formed by it.

---

# Formation Layers

## 1. Schuyler PSQ NASB 95 (Tuscany)

### Primary Reading Bible

This is my foundation for understanding, synthesis, and journaling.

Everything begins here.

My first engagement with Scripture happens before outside commentary or study resources influence my understanding.

---

## 2. Bullet Journal

### Personal Scripture Analysis

This is where I work through the text for myself.

Questions I return to:

- How do I understand what I am reading?
- What observations emerge?
- What questions remain?
- How does this shape my life and walk with God?

This is where Scripture moves from the page into personal reflection.

---

## 3. Hosanna Revival Compact NLT (Jerusalem Theme)

### Translation Comparison & Portable Reader

This is my porch and hiking Bible.

I use it to compare translations and clarify passages that are particularly difficult to understand in the NASB 95.

Its purpose is not to replace my primary reading Bible, but to provide another perspective on the text.

---

## 4. Baker Illustrated CSB Study Bible (Genuine Leather)

### Historical & Scholarly Study

This is reserved for deeper study at my desk.

It helps explore:

- Historical context.
- Cultural background.
- Archaeological information.
- Scholarly perspectives.

Importantly, this stage comes after my own reading and journaling so that outside interpretation does not shape my initial understanding of the passage.

---

## 5. NIV Lectio Bible (Practicing the Way / Zondervan)

### Meditative Reading

A true reader's Bible designed simply to become immersed in Scripture.

> "An ancient way to read the library of Scripture."

Its purpose is not analysis, but meditation and delight.

This creates space to slow down and remain with God's Word.

---

## 6. Local Church

### Corporate Formation

Scripture was never intended to be read only in isolation.

Through:

- Preaching.
- Corporate worship.
- Communion.
- Fellowship.
- The wisdom of other believers.

God continues to shape His people together.

This is where personal study meets the life of the Church.

---

## 7. Commonplace Book

### Long-Term Integration

This is where distilled ideas that stand the test of time are preserved.

Contents include:

- Favorite verses.
- Favorite quotes.
- Personal reflections.
- Enduring insights worth carrying throughout life.

Not everything encountered becomes something carried forward.

Some truths become markers.

---

## 8. Faithful Practice

### Daily Obedience

Knowledge becomes wisdom when it is lived.

This is where Scripture moves beyond the page into:

- Speech.
- Relationships.
- Work.
- Generosity.
- Forgiveness.
- Patience.
- Worship.
- Love.

Formation bears fruit as truth is lived.

---

# Prayer

Prayer is not another layer of study.

It is the posture that surrounds every layer.

Before reading, I ask for understanding.

During study, I ask for wisdom.

After reading, I ask for obedience.

Throughout the day, I ask to keep walking faithfully.

# Additional Study Resources

These resources serve as companions for understanding Scripture more deeply.

They are not replacements for Scripture, but tools that provide additional perspective and context.

---

## The Action Bible Study Bible ESV

### Visual Study & Biblical Overview

This resource provides a visual approach to Scripture, helping connect the larger biblical narrative through illustrations, summaries, and accessible explanations.

Its purpose is not to replace careful reading of Scripture, but to provide another perspective for understanding the story of the Bible as a whole.

---

## The Illustrated Guide to the Bible — J.R. Porter

### Historical & Visual Context

This resource provides historical background, cultural context, geography, and visual references that help illuminate the world of Scripture.

It serves as a companion for understanding the setting in which God's Word was written.

---

# Authors and Teachers Who Have Influenced My Thinking

These writings are not authorities over Scripture.

They are voices that have helped me think more carefully about faith, suffering, hope, discipleship, and life with God.

---

## Hope in Times of Fear — Timothy Keller

### Hope, Suffering, and the Character of God

This work has helped explore how Christian hope is rooted not in avoiding suffering, but in trusting God's character and promises through suffering.

---

## Courage to Stand — Russell Moore

### Faithfulness and Conviction

This work has helped examine what it means to remain faithful, humble, and compassionate while standing firmly in Christian conviction.

---

## Whispering of the Spirit — Dr. Drew Dickens

### Listening and Spiritual Formation

This work has contributed to reflection on recognizing God's presence, cultivating attentiveness, and growing in relationship with Him.

# My Formation Process

Looking back, this is the rhythm that gradually emerged in my own study.

1. Observation (NASB)
2. Personal Synthesis (Bullet Journal)
3. Translation Comparison (NLT)
4. Scholarly Synthesis (CSB Study Bible)
5. Meditative Reading (Lectio Bible)
6. Corporate Formation (Church)
7. Long-Term Integration (Commonplace Book)
8. Faithful Practice
9. Prayer
