# Hebrews 6 Reflection Example

This is an example of how The Guide may be used while sitting with a passage of Scripture.

The purpose is not to provide a complete interpretation of Hebrews 6.

It is to demonstrate a posture of reading:

Receive.

Understand.

Respond.

Remember.

Walk.

---

# Passage

## Hebrews 6:7-20

Key themes:

- Spiritual growth and fruitfulness.
- God's faithfulness.
- The certainty of His promises.
- Hope as an anchor for the soul.

---

# Observation

What do I notice?

The author uses the image of land receiving rain.

The same rain can produce different results depending on what the land bears.

The passage connects hearing God's Word with the fruit that follows.

The author also reminds believers that God remembers their work and love.

Later, the passage points to God's promise and oath, emphasizing that God cannot lie.

The result is a hope described as:

> "an anchor of the soul."

---

# Understanding

What is God communicating through this passage?

Hebrews is written to encourage believers to persevere in faith.

The warning passages throughout Hebrews are not invitations to live in fear, but calls to remain faithful and continue growing.

In this passage, the foundation of hope is not human strength.

The foundation is God's character.

God's promises are secure because God is faithful.

The anchor holds because of where it is attached.

---

# Reflection

What does this reveal about God?

God is faithful.

He remembers.

He keeps His promises.

He does not change.

---

What does this reveal about me?

I am dependent on God for growth.

Faithfulness requires continuing to walk, even when the entire path is not visible.

My hope cannot rest only on my circumstances or feelings.

---

# Quiet Whisper

What continues to echo after sitting with this passage?

Hope is not anchored in my ability to hold on.

Hope is anchored in God's faithfulness.

---

# Response

What is the next faithful step?

Continue returning to God's Word.

Continue trusting His promises.

Continue walking with the light He has provided.

Do not seek to see the entire path.

Trust God with the next step.

---

# Stone of Remembrance

A truth worth carrying forward:

God's promises are more secure than my circumstances.

When uncertainty comes, remember:

God cannot lie.

Hope is an anchor because God is faithful.

---
