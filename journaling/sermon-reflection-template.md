# Sermon Reflection Template

A sermon is an opportunity to receive teaching, examine Scripture, and consider how God may use His Word to shape us.

A sermon is not a replacement for personal engagement with Scripture.

The teaching we receive should always lead us back to God's Word.

This framework is not meant to capture every detail of a sermon.

It is a way to remember what was taught, reflect on its truth, and respond faithfully.

# Receive

Begin by receiving the message.

What was the central truth being taught?

What Scripture passages formed the foundation of the message?

What ideas, phrases, or illustrations stood out?

What did I notice?

The goal is not simply to record what was said.

The goal is to listen carefully.

---

# Teaching Notes

Record meaningful details from the sermon.

This may include:

- Important quotes
- Illustrations
- Historical or biblical context
- Questions raised
- Connections to Scripture
- Observations from the teacher

These notes serve as reminders of what was taught and provide material for further reflection.

---

# Understand

Consider the biblical truth being communicated.

Ask:

What does Scripture reveal about this topic?

What does this teach me about God?

What does this reveal about humanity?

How does this fit within the larger story of Scripture?

How does the message point me toward Christ?

A sermon should not only be heard.

It should be understood faithfully.

---

# Reflect

Allow the truth of the message to meet you personally.

Ask:

What challenged me?

What encouraged me?

What convicted me?

What did this reveal about my heart?

Where do I need to grow?

Where do I need to trust God?

Reflection begins with what God has revealed and considers how that truth should shape us.

---

# Respond

Consider how you should respond.

Ask:

What should I believe?

What should I surrender?

What should I change?

What should I practice?

What prayer comes from this message?

The goal is not simply to remember a sermon.

The goal is to allow God's Word to shape how we live.

---

# Bear Fruit

Consider what faithful living looks like.

Ask:

What is one step of obedience this truth invites me toward?

How should this affect my relationships?

How should this affect my thoughts, words, and actions?

How can this truth become visible in my life?

---

# Framework Connection

Connect this message to the larger work God is doing.

## Lamp to my feet

What light has God provided through this teaching?

How should it guide my next step?

## Left foot. Right foot.

What faithful step is before me?

How can I trust God with the path ahead?

## Receive → Understand → Respond

Did I simply hear this message, or am I allowing it to shape me?

---

# The Quiet Whisper

After reflecting on the sermon as a whole, what continues to echo?

The Quiet Whisper may be:

- A Scripture verse
- A phrase
- A truth
- An illustration
- A reminder of God's character

It is not the entire sermon summarized.

It is the truth that remains with you.

---

# The Stone of Remembrance

What do I want to carry forward?

What truth do I want to remember in a future season?

How has God used this message to teach, encourage, correct, or strengthen me?

The Stone is not a monument to the speaker or the sermon.

It is a reminder of God's faithfulness through His Word.

---

# Prayer

Respond to God.

Thank Him for what He has shown you.

Ask for help in living faithfully.

Surrender what needs to be surrendered.

Pray that His Word would continue its work in you.
