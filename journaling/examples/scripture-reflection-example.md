# Scripture Reflection Example

## Hebrews 5–6

>

# Receive

Hebrews continues explaining the superiority of Christ, but this section shifts toward spiritual maturity and perseverance.

The author describes Jesus as the ultimate High Priest, one who fully understands human weakness because He Himself experienced suffering and temptation.

A phrase that stood out was that Jesus "learned obedience from the things which He suffered."

This does not mean Jesus was ever disobedient.

Instead, it reveals that obedience was lived out through real suffering, faithfulness, and submission to the Father.

The warning passages in chapter 6 also stood out because they emphasize the seriousness of spiritual maturity.

The imagery of land receiving rain was especially powerful.

God provides what is needed, but the response of the soil reveals what is truly taking root.

---

# Understand

This passage reveals a God who is both holy and faithful.

The warning in Hebrews 6 is not meant to produce hopelessness, but to call believers toward maturity and endurance.

God's promises are secure because they rest on His character.

One of the most comforting truths in this passage is that God cannot lie.

Our hope is not anchored in our own ability to endure perfectly.

It is anchored in the faithfulness of the One who made the promise.

The author connects maturity with perseverance.

Faith is not simply a moment of belief.

It is a life of continuing to trust God.

---

# Respond

I want to remember that difficult seasons are not wasted.

God can use suffering to deepen obedience, strengthen faith, and produce maturity.

I want to continue moving forward rather than becoming discouraged when growth feels slow.

My confidence is not in my ability to hold onto God.

My confidence is in the reality that God is faithful and holds onto me.

---

# Bear Fruit

- Continue pursuing maturity rather than remaining spiritually passive.
- Trust God's promises when circumstances are difficult.
- Allow suffering to deepen faith rather than weaken it.
- Remember that hope is anchored in God's character.

# The Stone of Remembrance

_As The Guide developed, this practice was given a name._

At the time this entry was written, this truth was captured separately through a Scripture commonplace book.

This practice later became known as a Stone of Remembrance: a preserved truth that points back to God's faithfulness.

A Stone is not simply a favorite verse or interesting observation.

It is a truth worth carrying forward.

**Stone that emerged from this entry:**

My hope is not anchored in my ability to hold onto God.

My hope is anchored in the character and faithfulness of the God who cannot lie.
