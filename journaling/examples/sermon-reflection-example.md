# Sermon Information

**Date:**

**Title:** The Poison of Envy

**Speaker:**

**Passage(s):** 1 Corinthians 13:4

---

# Receive

The sermon focused on envy and the way comparison can distort the way we see ourselves, others, and God's work in our lives.

The foundation of the message came from 1 Corinthians 13:4:

> "Love is patient, love is kind and is not jealous; love does not brag and is not arrogant."

The message emphasized that envy is not simply wanting something someone else has.

At its core, envy reveals dissatisfaction with what God has given us and can cause us to view another person's blessing as something taken away from us.

The warning against envy is not only about our actions.

It is about the condition of our hearts.

---

# Teaching Notes

One illustration from the sermon stood out to me.

The pastor used the image of working in a coal mine.

A miner is not responsible for every section of the mine.

They are responsible for the section they have been given.

Their role is not to look at another miner's section and become frustrated that they have different conditions, different opportunities, or different results.

Their role is to faithfully work where they have been placed.

This image connected deeply with me because I am from the heart of coal country.

It is a familiar picture.

The lesson was not that we should stop growing or stop pursuing excellence.

The lesson was that faithfulness is not measured by comparison.

God has entrusted each person with different gifts, responsibilities, and circumstances.

---

# Understand

This sermon revealed that envy is ultimately a trust issue.

When I compare myself to others, I am often forgetting that God is the one who assigns gifts, opportunities, and callings.

Comparison shifts my focus away from God's faithfulness and toward what I believe I lack.

The answer to envy is not simply trying harder not to be jealous.

It is learning to trust God's wisdom and goodness.

The gifts God has given another person do not diminish what He has given me.

God's plan for someone else is not a threat to His plan for me.

---

# Reflect

This message challenged me to examine where comparison may be influencing my thoughts.

It reminded me that I do not need to compete with another child of God.

I need to faithfully walk the path God has given me.

There will always be someone who appears further ahead, more gifted, more successful, or more recognized.

But my calling is not to become someone else.

My calling is faithfulness.

---

# Respond

I want to practice gratitude instead of comparison.

I want to celebrate what God is doing in the lives of others rather than allowing their blessings to become sources of discontentment.

I want to focus on faithfully stewarding what God has entrusted to me.

My question should not be:

"Why did God give them that?"

My question should be:

"How can I faithfully use what God has given me?"

---

# Bear Fruit

- Celebrate the gifts and successes of others.
- Practice gratitude for what God has entrusted to me.
- Recognize comparison when it begins shaping my thoughts.
- Focus on faithfulness rather than measuring myself against others.
