# Bible Study Information

**Study:** 1 Peter Bible Study

**Passage(s):** 1 Peter

**Setting:** Group Bible Study

---

# Receive

This study focused on Peter's encouragement to believers learning how to live faithfully in the midst of difficulty.

Peter writes to believers who are experiencing pressure and hardship, reminding them of their identity in Christ and calling them toward holy living.

A theme that continued throughout the study was that faith is not merely something we claim to believe.

Faith shapes how we live.

Peter's message is not that suffering is meaningless.

It is that God is present and faithful even in difficult seasons.

---

# Discussion & Insights

One discussion that stood out was the difference between believing something is true and being convinced enough that it changes how we live.

It is possible to know the right answers without allowing those truths to transform our actions.

The group discussed the idea of examining the "rooms" of our hearts:

- What do we allow into our minds?
- What do we allow into our homes?
- What do we allow into our relationships?
- What do we allow to shape our desires?

This was a helpful reminder that spiritual growth is not only about what we say we believe.

It is about what we allow to influence us.

---

# Understand

Peter's writing reveals a God who calls His people into holiness while also providing the grace needed to grow.

Our identity comes before our obedience.

We do not pursue holiness in order to earn God's love.

We pursue holiness because we have already received His grace.

The study also reinforced that Scripture is not simply information to learn.

God's Word is meant to shape the way we think, the way we act, and the way we love others.

---

# Reflection

This study challenged me to consider whether my faith is simply something I affirm or something that actively shapes my life.

It is easy to say that I believe God's Word is true.

The deeper question is whether I am allowing that truth to change me.

Am I becoming more like Christ?

Am I allowing God access to every part of my life?

---

# Personal Reflection

One historical note from class stood out to me.

Dr. Rodney Stark's work, _The Rise of Christianity_, highlights how the early Church cared for abandoned children and elevated the dignity of women in ways that were profoundly counter-cultural.

Peter's instructions to husbands — including that failing to honor their wives hinders their prayers — fit within that larger picture of the Gospel transforming how people treat one another.

This reminds me that Christianity did not simply tell people to live differently.

It quietly reshaped an entire culture by changing hearts first.

---

# Respond

I want to remember that following Christ is not only about what I believe.

It is about surrendering every part of my life to Him.

I want my faith to move beyond agreement into obedience.

I want God's Word to shape my thoughts, relationships, and actions.

---

# Bear Fruit

- Examine what influences my heart and mind.
- Allow Scripture to shape daily decisions.
- Pursue holiness as a response to God's grace.
- Treat others with the dignity and love that reflects Christ.

# The Stone of Remembrance

_As The Guide developed, this practice was given a name._

At the time this entry was written, this truth was captured separately through a Scripture commonplace book.

This practice later became known as a Stone of Remembrance: a preserved truth that points back to God's faithfulness.

A Stone is not simply a favorite verse or interesting observation.

It is a truth worth carrying forward.

**Stone that emerged from this entry:**

God does not merely call His people to live differently.

Through His grace, He transforms hearts so that different living becomes possible.
