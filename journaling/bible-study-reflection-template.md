# Bible Study Reflection Template

A Bible study is an opportunity to study God's Word alongside others.

God often uses community, discussion, teaching, and shared study to deepen our understanding and encourage our faith.

This framework is not intended to capture every detail of a discussion.

It is a way to remember what was studied, consider what was learned, and reflect on how God is using His Word to shape us.

The goal is not simply to record what others said.

The goal is to faithfully understand and respond to what God has revealed through Scripture.

# Receive

Begin by receiving what was studied.

What passage was examined?

What was the main theme or truth discussed?

What stood out during the study?

What observations or questions emerged?

What did I notice that I may have missed on my own?

The goal is to listen carefully to God's Word and recognize how He may use others to help us understand it more deeply.

---

# Discussion & Insights

Record meaningful parts of the discussion.

Consider:

- Questions raised by the group
- Insights from other participants
- Different perspectives considered
- Connections to other passages
- Ideas that challenged or encouraged me

Community helps us see more clearly, but Scripture remains the authority.

The goal is not simply to collect opinions.

The goal is to seek understanding together under the authority of God's Word.

---

# Understand

Consider what the study revealed about the passage.

Ask:

What is the author communicating?

What does this reveal about God?

What does this reveal about humanity?

How does this fit within the larger story of Scripture?

What truths became clearer through study and discussion?

Seek understanding before application.

---

# Reflection

Allow the truth of Scripture to meet you personally.

Ask:

What challenged me?

What encouraged me?

What convicted me?

What questions remain?

What did this reveal about my heart?

What is God teaching me?

Reflection begins with what God has revealed and considers how that truth should shape me.

Reflection moves Scripture from something I studied into something I carry.

---

# Personal Reflection

Consider how this study connects with the broader work God is doing in your understanding, experience, and perspective.

This may include:

- Historical or cultural context that deepened understanding
- Connections to other areas of life
- Insights from discussion that changed how I see the passage
- Questions that continued beyond the study
- Thoughts that remained after the conversation ended

Ask:

What connections did this study reveal?

What perspectives changed or deepened?

What continued to make me think?

How does this help me see God's work more clearly?

Personal reflection does not replace the meaning of Scripture.

It is not a place to make the passage say something it does not say.

Instead, it is where faithful understanding connects with lived experience.

---

# Respond

Consider your response to what God has revealed.

Ask:

What should I believe?

What should I surrender?

What should I change?

What should I practice?

What prayer comes from this study?

The purpose of studying Scripture is not simply gaining knowledge.

It is becoming more faithful.

---

# Bear Fruit

Consider what obedience looks like.

Ask:

What does this truth look like lived out?

How should it affect my relationships?

How should it shape my character?

What is one faithful step I can take?

Spiritual growth is often formed through ordinary steps of obedience.

---

# Framework Connection

Connect this study to the larger work God is doing.

## Lamp to my feet

What light has God provided through this study?

How should it guide my next step?

## Left foot. Right foot.

What faithful step is before me?

How can I trust God with the path ahead?

## Receive → Understand → Respond

Is this truth simply something I discussed, or something I am allowing God to use to shape me?

---

# The Quiet Whisper

After sitting with the study as a whole, what continues to echo?

What verse, phrase, truth, or insight remains with me?

The Quiet Whisper is not simply the most interesting comment from the discussion.

It is the truth that remains after prayerful reflection.

The passage and the discussion provide the context.

The Whisper helps preserve what God brought to my attention.

---

# The Stone of Remembrance

What do I want to carry forward?

What truth do I want to remember?

How has God used this study, this passage, and this community to strengthen my faith?

The Stone is not a monument to myself.

It is a marker pointing back to God's faithfulness.

---

# Prayer

Respond to God.

Thank Him for His Word.

Thank Him for His people.

Ask for wisdom, humility, and obedience.

Pray that what was learned would become part of how you live.
