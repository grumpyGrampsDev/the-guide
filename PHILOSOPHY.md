# Philosophy

This guide exists to point people toward Scripture, not toward itself.

It is a companion, not a commentary.
It is a collection of principles, not a replacement for personal study.
Its purpose is not to provide answers to every question, but to encourage careful reading, thoughtful reflection, faithful prayer, and obedient living.
It is not meant to stand between the reader and Scripture, but to encourage the reader to return to Scripture again and again.

These principles emerged through the process of studying Scripture, journaling, asking questions, and learning to approach God's Word with humility.

As this guide grows, it will be shaped by the following principles.

---

## The Proper Place of This Guide

This guide cannot create faith, understanding, or spiritual growth.

Only God can do that.

Its purpose is simply to encourage faithful engagement with the means God has provided: His Word, prayer, the fellowship of believers, and the work of the Holy Spirit.

The Guide points.

God transforms.

---

## Scripture Is the Authority

Scripture is the final authority.

Every observation, principle, application, and conclusion in this guide must remain subject to God's Word.

If this guide ever disagrees with Scripture, Scripture is right.

Always.

---

## Christ Is the Goal

The goal of reading Scripture is not simply to gain knowledge.

The goal is to know Christ more deeply, to love Him more faithfully, and to become more like Him.

Growth in knowledge without growth in obedience is incomplete.

---

## Context Matters

Verses should never be separated from their context.

Chapters belong within books.
Books belong within the whole counsel of Scripture.
Individual passages should be understood in light of the author's intent and the unfolding story of redemption.

Scripture helps us understand Scripture. Individual passages should be read within the whole counsel of God's Word.

---

## Read Slowly

The Bible was not given merely to be finished.

It was given to be understood, believed, and lived.

There are seasons for reading broadly and seasons for lingering over a single paragraph.

Neither should be neglected.

---

## Observe Before Interpreting

Careful observation should come before interpretation.

Questions should come before conclusions.

The text should be allowed to speak before assumptions are brought to it.

---

## Application Flows from Truth

Application is essential, but it should grow naturally from a faithful understanding of the text.

The question is not first,

> "What does this mean to me?"

The first question is,

> "What did God intend to communicate through His Word?"

Only then should we ask how we ought to respond.

---

## Humility Matters

Every reader comes to Scripture with limitations.

This guide is written with the expectation that understanding grows over time.

Mistakes will be corrected.

Insights will deepen.

Better questions will be asked.

The guide itself should remain teachable.

---

## Simplicity Is a Virtue

Every sentence should earn its place.

If something can be said more clearly, it should be.

If something is unnecessary, it should be removed.

Clarity serves the reader.

---

## The Guide Is Never Finished

This repository is intentionally living.

As the Lord continues to teach, refine, convict, encourage, and mature me through His Word, this guide will continue to grow.

Its purpose is not to become complete.

Its purpose is to remain faithful.
