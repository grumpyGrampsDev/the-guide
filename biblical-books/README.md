# Biblical Books

## Opening Scripture

The Bible reading plan is the starting point for The Guide.

It exists to provide one possible path through Scripture with an emphasis on:

- Understanding context.
- Recognizing connections.
- Reading carefully.
- Responding faithfully.

This is not a race to complete the Bible.

It is an invitation to open God's Word with humility, attention, and a willingness to be formed by what He reveals.

---

# The Purpose of the Reading Plan

Scripture is not simply a collection of information to master.

It is God's Word to receive, understand, and live.

The goal of this plan is not:

- Completing Scripture as quickly as possible.
- Collecting knowledge.
- Measuring spiritual maturity.

The goal is:

- Knowing God through His Word.
- Seeing the story of redemption unfold.
- Learning to recognize His faithfulness.
- Taking the next faithful step in response.

---

The Bible reading plan begins the journey:

# Reading Philosophy

## Read Slowly

The goal is understanding, not speed.

Take time to notice:

- Repeated words.
- Themes.
- Questions.
- Connections.
- What the passage reveals about God.

---

## Understand Context

Scripture should be read with attention to:

- The author.
- The original audience.
- The purpose of the writing.
- The surrounding passage.
- The larger story of Scripture.

---

## Allow Scripture to Shape You

Reading Scripture is not only about gaining information.

It is about being formed.

Ask:

- What does this reveal about God?
- What does this reveal about humanity?
- What should I trust?
- What should I remember?
- How should I walk faithfully?

---

# The Reading Plan

The plan is divided into three parts:

## Part 1

Introduces the story of Jesus and the early Church, then moves through foundational Christian teaching.

## Part 2

Continues through the Old Testament foundations, Israel's history, and wisdom literature.

## Part 3

Completes the prophetic writings and provides practices for continuing to engage Scripture.
