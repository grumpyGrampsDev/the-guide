# Apply Faithfully

Reading Scripture is not complete when we understand what it says.

God's Word is meant to shape how we think, how we live, and who we are becoming.

The goal of Bible study is not simply information.

It is transformation.

Not transformation produced by our own effort, but transformation as God works through His Word.

This is something I have had to learn repeatedly. It is easy to measure growth by what I know, but Scripture continually brings me back to the question of how I am being shaped by what God has revealed.

## Understanding Leads to Response

A faithful reading of Scripture should lead somewhere.

Sometimes the response is repentance.

Sometimes it is worship.

Sometimes it is encouragement.

Sometimes it is patience.

Sometimes it is obedience in an area where God is calling us to grow.

The question is not only:

"What does this passage mean?"

It is also:

"How should I respond to what God has revealed?"

## Apply the Truth, Not Just the Details

Not every passage applies in exactly the same way.

Some commands are directly given to believers today.

Some passages reveal principles that require careful application.

Some passages remind us of God's character and faithfulness.

Faithful application requires understanding what the passage is actually communicating before deciding how it should shape our lives.

## Look for God's Character First

A common temptation is to immediately ask:

"What does this say about me?"

A better first question is:

"What does this reveal about God?"

Scripture reveals His character.

His holiness.

His faithfulness.

His mercy.

His justice.

His love.

Understanding who God is changes how we understand ourselves and how we respond to Him.

## Move From Knowledge to Obedience

Knowledge alone is not the goal.

A person can understand many truths about Scripture and remain unchanged.

God's Word invites a response.

Ask:

What should I believe?

What should I change?

What should I trust?

What should I surrender?

What should I practice?

What should I remember?

## Apply With Humility

Application is personal, but it is not merely subjective.

We do not ask Scripture to support what we already want.

We allow Scripture to shape what we want.

The question is not:

"How can I make this passage fit my life?"

The question is:

"How should my life be formed by this passage?"

## Faithfulness Is Often Found in Small Steps

Spiritual growth is usually not measured by one dramatic moment.

Often, it happens through ordinary faithfulness.

A prayer offered.

A sin confessed.

A habit changed.

A truth remembered.

A step of obedience taken.

God is faithful to work through the small steps of a life continually surrendered to Him.

## Remember What You Have Learned

Do not allow God's work in you to become a forgotten moment.

Write it down.

Pray over it.

Return to it.

Remembering God's faithfulness strengthens us for future seasons.

The lessons God teaches us become reminders of His continued presence and care.

## A Final Encouragement

The purpose of reading Scripture is not simply to make us better informed.

It is to make us more faithful.

Read carefully.

Understand humbly.

Respond obediently.

Trust God with the growth.

His Word is faithful.
