# Read Slowly

There is a natural temptation to measure Bible reading by quantity.

How many chapters did I finish?

How many books have I completed?

How many times have I read through the Bible?

Those questions are not unimportant, but they are not the most important ones.

Over time, I have found that some of the passages I remember most clearly were not the ones I read the fastest, but the ones I returned to slowly and repeatedly.

A better question is this:

**Did I truly listen to what God said?**

## The Bible Is Not a Race

There are seasons for reading large portions of Scripture.

There are also seasons for spending an entire morning in a handful of verses.

Neither approach is inherently more spiritual.

The goal is not speed.

The goal is understanding.

Reading slowly gives us room to notice what hurried reading often misses.

## Notice Before Moving

Repeated words.

Unexpected phrases.

Questions.

Commands.

Connections to earlier passages.

Images.

Promises.

Warnings.

The Bible rewards careful attention.

Many of its treasures are found by readers who pause long enough to notice them.

## Sit With Difficult Passages

Not every verse yields its meaning immediately.

Some passages require patience.

Read them again.

Read the surrounding context.

Look at what came before.

Notice what comes after.

Sometimes the greatest growth comes not from finding an immediate answer, but from learning to remain with the question.

## Let Scripture Shape Your Pace

Some narratives invite long stretches of reading.

Poetry often invites lingering.

Paul's letters reward careful, deliberate thought.

Wisdom literature asks to be pondered.

There is no single pace that fits every passage.

Read each genre in the way it invites you to be read.

## Reflection Is Part of Reading

Closing the Bible is not the end of reading.

Carry the passage with you.

Think about it while driving.

While working.

While walking.

Return to it in prayer.

Some of the deepest lessons from Scripture are learned hours after we have finished reading.

## Slow Reading Is Not Lazy Reading

Reading slowly is not an excuse to avoid reading broadly.

It is a reminder that God's Word is meant to be understood, believed, and obeyed—not merely completed.

Sometimes faithfulness looks like ten chapters.

Sometimes it looks like one verse that stays with you all day.

Both can be acts of worship.

## A Final Encouragement

Do not feel pressured to hurry through God's Word.

It has endured through generations, and it is still speaking today.

Its truth will not become less valuable because we choose to spend another five minutes listening carefully.

Open your Bible.

Slow down.

Listen well.

## God is speaking.

## Put It Into Practice

Choose a short passage of Scripture and read it at least three times. With each reading, resist the urge to move ahead. Instead, allow yourself to linger over the words, paying attention to anything that stands out or invites further reflection.
