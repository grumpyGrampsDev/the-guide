# How to Approach Scripture

Every time we open the Bible, we are doing something remarkable.

We are opening the written revelation of the God who created all things.

That reality alone should shape how we read.

Not with fear, but with reverence.

Not with anxiety, but with expectation.

Not merely to gather information, but to know the One who has spoken.

Over time, I have found that the way we approach Scripture often shapes what we are able to receive from it.

Before asking what a passage means, it is worth asking another question.

**How should I come to it?**

## Come Humbly

No one approaches Scripture knowing everything.

Every reader has blind spots, assumptions, experiences, and limitations.

Humility recognizes that God does not need to adjust His Word to fit us.

We need to be shaped by His Word.

The humble reader is willing to be corrected.

Willing to admit, "I hadn't seen that before."

Willing to replace long-held assumptions with what Scripture actually says.

## Come Prayerfully

The Bible is not merely understood through intellectual effort alone.

Understanding is a gift from God.

Prayer reminds us that we are dependent upon Him—not only for salvation, but for wisdom, conviction, encouragement, and growth.

Before reading, ask God to help you see what He intends you to see.

Ask Him to reveal truth.

Ask Him to expose sin.

Ask Him to deepen your love for Christ.

Ask Him to help you obey whatever you learn.

## Come Attentively

The Bible deserves our attention.

Read slowly enough to notice repeated words.

Notice questions.

Notice commands.

Notice promises.

Notice contrasts.

Notice what surprises you.

Many treasures of Scripture are not missed because they are impossible to find.

They are overlooked because we hurry past them.

## Come Honestly

There will be passages you understand immediately.

There will also be passages that confuse you.

Both are normal.

Resist the temptation to pretend certainty where you have questions.

God is not threatened by honest questions asked with humble hearts.

Write them down.

Return to them later.

Often, Scripture answers Scripture in time.

## Come Expectantly

God has promised that His Word accomplishes His purposes.

Sometimes that work is immediate.

Sometimes it is quiet and gradual.

Sometimes you will leave a reading amazed.

Other days you may simply leave encouraged to take one more faithful step.

Both are evidence of God's work.

Never underestimate what the Lord can accomplish through ordinary, consistent time in His Word.

## Come Ready to Respond

Bible reading is not complete when the chapter ends.

God's Word invites a response.

Sometimes that response is worship.

Sometimes repentance.

Sometimes gratitude.

Sometimes trust.

Sometimes obedience.

The goal is not simply to finish reading.

The goal is to leave more willing to believe what God has said and to live accordingly.

## A Simple Reminder

Before you open your Bible, remember this:

Come humbly.

Come prayerfully.

Come attentively.

Come honestly.

Come expectantly.

Come ready to respond.

Some days these postures will come naturally. Other days it will be something we intentionally return to.

Then begin reading.

God has already spoken.
