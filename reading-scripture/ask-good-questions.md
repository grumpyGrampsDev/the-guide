# Ask Good Questions

One of the most valuable habits a student of Scripture can develop is learning to ask good questions.

Questions slow us down.

They keep us from assuming.

They invite us to listen before we explain.

A question does not mean you doubt God's Word.

Often, it means you are finally paying attention.

Over time, I have found that the questions I bring to Scripture often reveal how carefully I am actually listening to it.

## Start with Simple Questions

Not every question needs to be profound.

Sometimes the simplest questions uncover the deepest truths.

Who is speaking?

Who is being addressed?

What happened?

What command is being given?

What promise is being made?

What is repeated?

What surprised me?

These simple observations become the foundation for deeper understanding.

## Ask Why

When something catches your attention, ask why.

Why is this repeated?

Why did the author include this detail?

Why does the passage begin here?

Why does it end this way?

Why is this command connected to what came before?

The Bible was written intentionally.

Questions help us discover that intentionality.

## Follow the Author's Thought

Rather than collecting isolated verses, follow the flow of the passage.

Ask questions like:

How did the author arrive at this conclusion?

What is this word connecting?

What problem is being addressed?

How does one paragraph lead into the next?

Scripture is not merely a collection of truths.

It is a collection of carefully written books.

Read them that way.

## Welcome Difficult Questions

Eventually you will encounter verses you cannot explain.

Do not be discouraged.

Write the question down.

Keep reading.

Study the context.

Return later.

Sometimes the answer is only a few verses away.

Sometimes it is in another book of the Bible.

Sometimes it comes months or years later.

Patience is part of faithful study.

## Beware of the Wrong Questions

Some questions seek understanding.

Others simply seek arguments.

Ask questions that help you know God more faithfully, understand His Word more accurately, and obey Him more completely.

Curiosity is a gift.

Use it wisely.

## Let Questions Lead You to Worship

The goal is not to collect an endless list of unanswered questions.

The goal is to let each question draw you further into the riches of God's Word.

Some questions will receive clear answers.

Others will leave you marveling at the wisdom of God.

Both are valuable.

The Bible is deep enough to reward a lifetime of thoughtful questions.

## A Final Encouragement

Do not fear your questions.

Bring them to Scripture.

Bring them to prayer.

Bring them to trusted believers.

Then keep reading.

God often answers sincere questions through the steady, faithful study of His Word.
