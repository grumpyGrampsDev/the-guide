# Stone of Remembrance Example

## Prayer and Season

> This is an example of a Stone of Remembrance emerging from a season of prayer, uncertainty, and learning to trust God.
>
> A Stone is not a record of a difficult circumstance alone. It is a reminder of God's faithfulness within that circumstance.

---

# Origin

**Source:** Prayer and Season of Life

During a season where the future I had expected changed dramatically, I found myself having to reconsider many things I thought I understood about my life.

A serious relationship that included a shared future came to an end.

Soon after, my family received difficult news that introduced new uncertainties and responsibilities I had not anticipated.

The future felt unclear.

Plans I had been building toward were no longer certain.

During that season, I reached a point where I stopped trying to be the director of my own life.

I called out to God for guidance.

I opened His Word with an open heart, not looking for a way to control the outcome, but seeking to know Him and follow where He led.

---

# The Truth

When I surrender my need to control the path ahead, God provides the light needed for the next step.

His Word is a lamp for my feet, not because it reveals every detail of the journey, but because it faithfully guides me as I walk.

---

# Why This Became a Stone

Before this season, I often approached life by trying to understand and plan the path ahead.

But uncertainty revealed my limits.

I could not control every circumstance.

I could not see every future responsibility.

I could not solve every unknown.

What I could do was open God's Word.

What I could do was pray.

What I could do was take the next faithful step.

God did not give me the entire map.

He gave me enough light to walk.

---

# The Stone

I do not need to know the entire path before I take the next faithful step.

God provides the light.

My calling is to walk faithfully in it.

---

# Looking Back

When future circumstances feel uncertain, I can return to this truth:

God is not asking me to carry tomorrow's entire path today.

He is faithful with today's step.

His Word remains a lamp.

His presence remains steady.

His grace remains sufficient.

---

Lamp to my feet.

Left foot.

Right foot.
