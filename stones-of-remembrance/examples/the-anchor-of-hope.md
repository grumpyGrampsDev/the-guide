# Stone of Remembrance Example

## Hebrews 5–6

> This is an example of a Stone of Remembrance emerging from a personal Scripture reflection.
>
> A Stone is not created for every entry. It is a truth that remains and points back to God's faithfulness.
>
> Some truths are meaningful in a moment. Some truths become markers we return to over time.

---

# Origin

**Source:** Personal Scripture Reflection

**Passage:** Hebrews 5–6

During this reading, several truths stood out:

- Jesus understands human weakness because He experienced suffering and temptation.
- Spiritual maturity is formed through endurance and faithful obedience.
- God cannot lie.
- Our hope is an anchor because it rests in God's character.

The Quiet Whisper from this entry was:

> "This hope we have as an anchor of the soul, a hope both sure and steadfast..."

— Hebrews 6:19 (NASB 1995)

---

# The Truth

My hope is not anchored in my ability to hold onto God.

My hope is anchored in the character and faithfulness of the God who cannot lie.

---

# Why This Became a Stone

Hebrews reminded me that faith is not sustained by my own strength.

Spiritual maturity is not formed through avoiding difficulty, but through learning to trust God through it.

When circumstances feel uncertain, my confidence cannot rest in my feelings, my understanding, or my ability to remain steady.

God's promises are secure because His character is unchanging.

The anchor is not the strength of my grip.

The anchor is the One who holds me.

---

# The Stone

God's faithfulness is my anchor.

My hope does not depend on my ability to hold onto Him perfectly.

My hope rests in the God who is faithful, who cannot lie, and who keeps His promises.

---

# Looking Back

When future seasons feel uncertain, I can return to this truth:

God has not changed.

The same God who was faithful before remains faithful now.

My circumstances may shift.

My feelings may change.

My understanding may be incomplete.

But the character of God remains the same.

---

Lamp to my feet.

Left foot.

Right foot.
